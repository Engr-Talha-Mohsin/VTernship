Cookie Policy

How Vternship uses cookies and similar technologies

Last Updated February 13, 2026 Version 2.1.0

This Cookie Policy explains how Vternship, Inc. ("Vternship," "we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website, platform, and applications (collectively, the "Services"). It explains what these technologies are, why we use them, and your rights to control our use of them.

Quick Summary: We use cookies to make our platform work, enhance your experience, analyze how you use our services, and personalize content. You can control most cookies through your browser settings or our Cookie Preference Center below. Essential cookies are necessary for the platform to function and cannot be disabled.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners. Cookies help us remember your preferences, understand how you use our platform, and personalize your experience.

Cookies can be classified by:

  • Duration: Session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent cookies (remain on your device until they expire or you delete them).
  • Source: First-party cookies (set by Vternship) or third-party cookies (set by our trusted partners).

Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies:

  • Web Beacons/Pixel Tags: Tiny graphics with unique identifiers that track user behavior and email engagement.
  • Local Storage: Browser-based storage (HTML5 localStorage and sessionStorage) that allows us to store data locally on your device.
  • SDKs: Software Development Kits used in our mobile applications to collect usage data and enable features.
  • Fingerprinting: Collection of device attributes to create a unique identifier (used only for security purposes).

For simplicity, we refer to all these technologies collectively as "cookies" throughout this policy.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several important purposes:

Essential/Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are required for the platform to function and cannot be disabled. They enable core functionality such as:

  • User authentication and session management
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Load balancing and server performance
  • Compliance with legal obligations

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our platform by collecting anonymous information. We use this data to:

  • Count visits and traffic sources
  • Identify which pages are most and least popular
  • Track user journeys through the platform
  • Measure and improve platform performance
  • Identify and fix bugs and errors

Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization:

  • Remembering your login information
  • Saving your preferences and settings
  • Remembering your search filters
  • Enabling chat and messaging features
  • Storing items in your saved internships list

Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies may be set through our platform by our advertising partners to:

  • Build a profile of your interests
  • Show you relevant advertisements on other sites
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Measure the effectiveness of ad campaigns

Note: Vternship does not currently serve targeted advertisements but may use third-party retargeting cookies for marketing purposes.

5. How to Control Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can typically:

  • View and delete individual cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all third-party cookies
  • Clear all cookies when you close the browser

For specific instructions, visit your browser's help documentation:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of certain third-party cookies through industry opt-out tools:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) Opt-Out
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) Opt-Out
  • Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on

5.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers have "Do Not Track" (DNT) features that signal to websites that you do not want your online activity tracked. Our platform does not currently respond to DNT signals. We continue to work with the online industry to develop a common understanding of how to treat DNT signals.

5.4 Mobile Applications

For our mobile applications, you can control tracking through your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings > Privacy > Tracking
  • Android: Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization

6. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our use of cookies, legal requirements, or operational practices. When we make material changes, we will notify you by:

  • Posting a prominent notice on our platform
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy
  • Sending an email notification (for significant changes)

We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about our use of cookies and similar technologies.
